# Diary Revision Checklist

Score each dimension from 1 to 5. Revise anything below 4.

## 1) Authenticity

- Facts match the source text.
- No invented people/events/details.
- First-person voice still sounds like the user.

## 2) Structure

- Entry has one clear central thread.
- Paragraph order serves meaning, not just time order.
- Each paragraph has one memory point.

## 3) Specificity

- Concrete details exist (place, object, amount, dialogue, action).
- Vague claims are supported by at least one scene.
- Empty summary lines are removed.

## 4) Reflection Depth

- At least one "why this matters" sentence appears.
- Reflection is grounded in events, not slogans.
- Emotional tone is controlled and believable.

## 5) Readability

- Run-on sentences are split.
- Repetitive connectors are reduced.
- Transitions are smooth with contrast patterns where helpful.

## 6) Ending Quality

- Ending includes one specific action.
- Action is realistic and time-bound.
- Ending avoids generic motivation phrases.

## Fast Fix Playbook

If score is low, apply targeted fixes:

- Low authenticity -> restore original facts and wording markers.
- Low structure -> merge/split paragraphs by intent.
- Low specificity -> inject concrete detail from source notes.
- Low reflection -> add one interpretation line after key scene.
- Low readability -> shorten long lines and remove repeats.
- Low ending quality -> rewrite final line as a practical commitment.

## One-Pass Prompt Fragment

Use this fragment during polishing:

"Revise the diary entry with this checklist. Keep facts unchanged and voice authentic. Prioritize structure, specificity, and reflection depth. Then output: (1) revised text, (2) checklist scores, (3) three surgical edits made."
